Exhibit 1

Three messages matter for a May market readout: system balance, Tokyo exposure, and data confidence.

The strongest evidence comes from processed public JEPX offer-stack/intraday data and Tokyo/Kansai generation mix. Fuel, SRMC, futures, and weather remain sample/proxy screens.

What is solid

System-level scarcity was limited

  • System stack had 2 scarcity blocks and 84 thin blocks across 4,176 records.
  • Average system clearing estimate was 14.20 JPY/kWh.
  • Implication: no broad May scarcity narrative.
What is actionable

Tokyo remained the higher-risk shape

  • Tokyo residual thermal share was 58.6% versus Kansai at 34.7%.
  • Tokyo evening thermal ramp averaged 5.8 GW versus Kansai at 1.9 GW.
  • Implication: monitor Tokyo ramp blocks into summer.
What needs caution

Fuel and futures are screening inputs

  • Sample JKM fell 10.2% m/m and coal fell 12.8% m/m.
  • Power screened above SRMC proxies.
  • Implication: load vendor curves before trade-level conclusions.
So what: the May desk discussion should focus less on average price direction and more on where stack depth, intraday ranges, and Tokyo residual thermal exposure line up.
Source: Dashboard May 2026 metrics; JEPX public offer-stack and intraday datasets; processed JapanesePower.org Tokyo/Kansai generation mix.

Exhibit 7

Near-term watchlist: move from monthly averages to block-level operating stress.

The revised dashboard should help a power analyst identify where market structure could matter before it shows up in daily average prices.

Priority 1

Tokyo ramp risk

  • Track Tokyo residual thermal share and evening ramp daily.
  • Combine with temperature and intraday ranges.
  • Flag repeated premium widening versus Kansai.
Priority 2

Offer-stack shift

  • Compare latest block curve against 7-day and 30-day benchmarks.
  • Show sell-depth and buy-depth shifts around clearing.
  • Separate System, East/Tokyo, and West/Kansai groups.
Priority 3

Curve and fuel validation

  • Replace sample futures and fuel curves with vendor or broker marks.
  • Keep SRMC bands but label methodology and units.
  • Connect news tags to nuclear, solar, thermal outages, and grid constraints.
Source: Analyst synthesis from May dashboard readout and current data-pipeline limitations.
